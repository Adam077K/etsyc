# 02 — Stated vs. Revealed: The Say-Do Gap in Authentic / Local / Handmade Consumption

**Researched:** 2026-07-15
**Question:** How real and how large is the attitude–behavior (say-do) gap for authentic / local / handmade / ethical consumption, and what actually closes it at the point of purchase?
**Confidence overall:** MEDIUM-HIGH on the size and shape of the gap; MEDIUM on the mechanisms that reliably close it; LOW on causal effect sizes (most conversion cases are observational, not experimental).

---

## TL;DR

1. The gap is real and large across every category tested. In behavioral studies, roughly **40-80%** of stated purchase intent does not translate to actual purchase. Category-specific say-do gaps: auto **53%**, grocery **33%**, travel **28%**; a Veylinx-style bidding study showed 83% claimed interest vs 42% actual bids (a ~49-point absolute drop, ~81% relative gap).
2. Sustainable / ethical / local premiums are *stated* generously (54-80%+ say they will pay more; PwC 2024 puts the average willingness at **9.7%**) but revealed premiums evaporate under price comparison — especially for Gen Z, where **91%** cite price as the dominant online purchase driver and **32%** of self-identified eco-conscious Gen Z have recently bought from Shein.
3. There is a robust **"struggle premium" for hand-made / human-authored** work (~59% will pay 20-30% more; ~73% pay more for human vs. AI creative output; certified handmade gets 20-150% higher margins) — but this premium is fragile, category-specific, and requires strong proof at the decision moment.
4. What closes the gap is **not stronger values messaging**. It is (a) proof-of-authenticity at the buy moment (authentication, verification, provenance), (b) format changes that convert values into impulse (live drops, creator endorsement, scarcity), and (c) price-parity narrowing so the values decision no longer competes with a materially cheaper dupe.

## Section 1 — Size and shape of the say-do gap

### 1a. The gap is measured, not anecdotal

| Claim | Source | Date | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~38% of US online shoppers do not actually follow their previously stated behavior; 38% inaccurately recall their online behaviors | [Digiday summary of behavioral tracking study](https://digiday.com/media/new-study-reveals-large-gaps-in-what-consumers-think-they-do-online-versus-what-they-actually-do/) | ~2023 | MED |
| Category-specific say-do gaps: **auto 53%, grocery 33%, travel 28%** | [Digiday](https://digiday.com/media/new-study-reveals-large-gaps-in-what-consumers-think-they-do-online-versus-what-they-actually-do/) / Kantar | ~2023 | MED |
| In one bidding-based study: **83% claimed interest** in a product; only **42% actually placed a bid** — average gap of ~81% between claim and behavior | [Veylinx blog citing their own behavioral panel](https://veylinx.com/blog/how-big-is-the-gap-between-what-consumers-say-and-do) | 2023-2024 | MED (vendor-published; direction is well-corroborated in literature) |
| In ethical consumption specifically, 30% of self-declared "green" consumers report being very concerned but struggle to translate concern into purchase; Finnish n=713 study confirms importance-of-ethics does not predict purchase | [Casais & Faria, SAGE 2022](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/02761467211054836); [Value–Action Gap Wikipedia summary of primary studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Value-action_gap) | 2022 | HIGH (peer-reviewed) |
| Major moderators of the ethical intention–behavior gap: information difficulty, product availability, **high price of ethical products** | Casais & Faria 2022 (above) | 2022 | HIGH |

### 1b. Why this gap exists (mechanisms from literature)

- **Social desirability bias:** consumers over-report virtuous intent to interviewers.
- **Present bias:** in-the-moment price comparison beats abstract values.
- **Habits & defaults** mediate more of the intention-behavior link than attitudes do (Casais & Faria).
- **Sacrifice moderator:** premium size is the single strongest predictor of whether intent converts.

**Implication:** Any product built on "people say they want local/handmade/real" is building on the *most inflated* part of the survey stack. Design should assume the gap and defeat it structurally.

## Section 2 — Willingness-to-pay premium for handmade / authentic / real

### 2a. Stated premiums (be skeptical)

| Claim | Source | Date | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|

| **~90%** of consumers will pay more for something from a brand they trust (n=1,800) | [Salsify consumer research](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/consumers-will-pay-a-premium-for-brands-they-trust-salsify-finds-in-new-consumer-research-301238179.html) | 2021 | MED |

### 2b. Revealed fragility of the premium

- Etsy — the closest large-market proxy for "handmade premium" — saw **GMS decline ~5% to $11.9B in 2025** vs. $12.6B in 2024, with buyer count dropping to 86.5M and management citing "pressure on consumer discretionary spending and evolving buyer behavior." Revenue only grew 2.7% and only because Etsy pushed **take-rate to a record 23.3% in Q1 2025**. Source: [Etsy FY2025 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1370637/000137063726000019/etsy-20251231.htm), [Investing.com SWOT summary](https://www.investing.com/news/swot-analysis/etsys-swot-analysis-handmade-marketplace-stock-faces-growth-challenges-93CH-4278000). **HIGH confidence.**
- Interpretation: the handmade premium exists, but the *volume* the premium can support is being ground down by macro price pressure. Etsy is monetizing a shrinking pool of committed buyers rather than converting new ones from mass retail.

### 2c. What this means

The premium is real *when the buyer is already inside a handmade-context environment* (Etsy shop, craft fair, direct-from-maker). It largely evaporates in *head-to-head comparison* against a mass-market or dupe alternative on a general shopping surface. This is the fundamental positioning problem.

### 3c. Interpretation

Gen Z is not hypocritical — they are *rationally strategic under budget constraints*. Their values are real but non-binding; their price sensitivity is real and binding. "Real / handmade / local" positioning without a delivery mechanism at price parity or with strong compensatory value will not convert them.

## Section 4 — What actually converts stated preference into purchase

None of the following are silver bullets in isolation. The evidence supports these as *mechanisms that have measurably shifted revealed behavior in specific contexts*.

### 4a. Proof of authenticity at the decision moment (highest leverage)

- **StockX / GOAT authentication:** the entire ~$10B+ sneaker resale market runs on the fact that the marketplace verifies before ship. The premium buyers pay above raw peer-to-peer prices *is* the authentication insurance. Case source: [Harvard D3 Digital Innovation case, "StockX: The price of trust"](https://d3.harvard.edu/platform-digit/submission/stockx-the-price-of-trust/). **HIGH confidence** that authentication converts high WTP into actual transactions when the alternative is uncertain provenance.
- **Certified handmade** marks reportedly yield 20-150% higher margins ([HMG Pop Up](https://hmgpopup.com/why-handmade-certification-matters-securing-product-authenticity-and-margins/)) — LOW source quality, but directionally consistent with the StockX pattern.
- **Mechanism:** removes the risk that "real" is just marketing. Turns a values claim into a verifiable spec.

### 4b. Format changes that make values into impulse

- **Live commerce** converts at **3-10x traditional ecommerce** (10-30% vs. 2-5%) by bundling product demo + social proof + scarcity + direct CTA in one moment. Source: [Conbersa live commerce benchmarks](https://www.conbersa.ai/learn/live-commerce-roi-benchmarks); [Opensend live stream stats](https://www.opensend.com/post/live-stream-conversion-rate-statistics-ecommerce). **MED confidence** (vendor benchmarks; direction is confirmed across many sources).
- **Whatnot:** $6B+ 2025 sales, more than doubled YoY; buyers spend **~80 min/day** on platform. Categories now span fashion, beauty, toys — not just collectibles. Source: [Whatnot 2026 trends](https://blog.teamwhatnot.com/unitedstates/2026trends). **HIGH confidence** on scale.
- **Depop:** ~$1B 2025 sales, ~60% YoY growth in US, ~90% of buyers under 34, 7M active. Source: [Fast Company / Inc summary of parent eBay disclosure](https://www.inc.com/fast-company-2/depop-might-be-the-last-resale-app-not-drowning-in-ai-generated-junk-images-is-that-why-gen-z-loves-it/91306427). **MED-HIGH confidence.**
- **Vintage-on-live** — vintage resellers moving to Whatnot / eBay Live report booming auction economics; the ephemerality of live + one-of-one nature of vintage is a natural fit. Source: [Glossy "why vintage sellers are taking resale live"](https://www.glossy.co/fashion/why-vintage-sellers-are-taking-resale-live/). **MED confidence** (trade press, not measured causally).

### 4c. Creator endorsement, but *authentic* not paid-look

- **TikTok Shop** average conversion **~4.7%** vs. traditional ecommerce 2-4% and other social 1.9%. Source: [Coozmoo / eMarketer citation](https://coozmoo.com/blogs/social-media/tiktok-creators-convert-better-than-other-platforms). **MED confidence.**
- **Clinique TikTok influencer campaign:** reported **+441% conversion rate lift** from letting creators authentically demo product. Source: same. **LOW-MED confidence** (single campaign, brand-reported; directionally corroborated).
- Nano/micro-influencer authenticity beats macro reach for conversion in most consumer categories — pattern is well-corroborated in influencer benchmarking (Influencer Marketing Hub, Shopify).
- **Mechanism:** the creator functions as a *trusted human proof of realness* — the same job "certified handmade" does in a marketplace, delivered through a person Gen Z already parasocially trusts.

### 4d. Scarcity and drops — but only if authentic

- Live commerce urgency (limited-time offers, drops, real-time inventory) is a core conversion driver. Source: [Conbersa](https://www.conbersa.ai/learn/live-commerce-roi-benchmarks).
- **Critical caveat:** false scarcity damages trust and reduces repeat engagement. Same source, plus PMC study on scarcity backfire on green products: [PMC 11037426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11037426/). **MED confidence.** For green/values products specifically, poorly-signaled scarcity can *decrease* purchase intent.
- **Mechanism:** scarcity accelerates decision under buyer's own values; it does not substitute for the values.

### 4e. Friction removal & price-parity narrowing

- Casais & Faria (2022) identify **plans and habits** as the mediators between intention and behavior. That is a friction-removal claim: any design that pre-commits, subscribes, one-clicks, or reminds the values-oriented buyer closes gap.
- If price gap to the dupe is >30-40%, no amount of storytelling closes the gap for the mainstream Gen Z shopper (implied by 91% price-primacy stat and Simon-Kucher's 6-point sustainability importance decline under inflation).

## Section 5 — Counter-cases where a realness/authenticity premium DOES reliably convert

| Case | Why it converts | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **StockX / GOAT authentication marketplaces** — buyers *pay a fee* for verification and consistently accept a premium above raw resale peer-to-peer. | Authentication solves the information-asymmetry problem; without it the premium collapses. See [Harvard D3 case](https://d3.harvard.edu/platform-digit/submission/stockx-the-price-of-trust/). | HIGH |
| **Whatnot live auctions for vintage / collectibles** — $6B GMV, one-of-one items sell above list because bidding pressure + creator host + provable scarcity converge. | Values (rarity, provenance, story) are demonstrated *live* and bounded in time. Buyers cannot defer to comparison-shop. Source: [Whatnot 2026 trends](https://blog.teamwhatnot.com/unitedstates/2026trends). | HIGH on scale, MED on causal claim |
| **Depop vintage/secondhand** — Gen Z demonstrably pays for one-of-one used items when the platform shows the seller as a real person with taste. | Combines resale price sanity + curator-as-person + no-two-alike scarcity. Source: [Fast Company/Inc](https://www.inc.com/fast-company-2/depop-might-be-the-last-resale-app-not-drowning-in-ai-generated-junk-images-is-that-why-gen-z-loves-it/91306427). | MED-HIGH |
| **Drops (Supreme, sneaker collabs, Aimé Leon Dore, etc.)** — well-covered in trade press: fixed windows + scarcity + authenticity guarantee + community identity produce premiums the same product would not command in an always-on catalog. | Time-boundedness eliminates "I'll compare later"; community identity gives the purchase a social payoff beyond the object. | MED (industry consensus, sparse controlled evidence) |
| **Direct-from-maker at craft fairs / farmers' markets** — the maker themselves is the proof; buyers report higher WTP in-person vs. same item online. | The person-as-proof is present *at the decision moment*; no dupe-comparison surface is available. | LOW-MED (evidence is largely qualitative) |
| **TikTok Shop beauty via creator demo** (Clinique-type cases) — creator authenticity + native checkout at the moment of persuasion. | Cuts the multi-tab price-comparison step out of the funnel entirely. Source: [Coozmoo](https://coozmoo.com/blogs/social-media/tiktok-creators-convert-better-than-other-platforms). | MED |

### Common pattern across all counter-cases

1. **Proof of realness is present at the exact decision moment**, not just in the brand story.
2. **The comparison-to-dupe step is structurally removed** (live time-bound, one-of-one, verified rare, or in-person).
3. **The buyer gets social payoff, not just the object** (community identity, taste signalling, gifting story).

If any of the three is missing, the premium leaks.

## Section 6 — Gaps / UNKNOWN

- **Direct causal effect sizes** of "seeing the maker" on conversion at Etsy-like general-catalog surfaces — no published RCT found. All evidence is observational or platform-reported.
- **Actual paid premium for "made by a real person" claims vs. identical mass-produced items in controlled A/B tests** — the Struggle Premium arXiv paper is the closest, but it tests AI-vs-human, not handmade-vs-factory in commerce contexts.
- **Depop / Whatnot cohort economics** — how much of the growth is genuine values-driven demand vs. cheap-secondhand demand? Public numbers do not break this down.
- **Long-term retention** of live-commerce buyers on Whatnot / TikTok Shop — the format converts but 2026 emarketer data shows TikTok Shop *livestream share* dropped from 26% (Jul 2024) to 18% (Jul 2025), suggesting format fatigue may be real. Source: [emarketer live shopping FAQ 2026](https://www.emarketer.com/content/faq-on-livestream-commerce--what-marketers-need-know-about-live-shopping-2026).
- **Whether the 9.7% PwC premium holds at the individual transaction level for handmade goods specifically** — PwC studies sustainability broadly, not handmade.
- **Regional variation** in the say-do gap for local/handmade — most cited studies are US/EU; developing-market patterns may differ.

---

## Section 7 — Confidence summary

- **Size of gap:** HIGH — multiple independent sources, peer-reviewed and industry, converge on 30-80% intention-behavior gap depending on category.
- **Premium exists in-context, fragile in comparison:** HIGH — Etsy 10-K + Simon-Kucher + PwC align.
- **Gen Z price-primacy vs. stated values:** HIGH — multiple 2024-25 sources; the Shein/Temu behavioral data is unambiguous.
- **Live commerce conversion:** MED — vendor benchmarks dominate; direction is confirmed but exact multipliers vary.
- **Authentication marketplaces converting realness premium:** HIGH on existence, MED on effect size vs. counterfactual.
- **Creator endorsement / TikTok Shop conversion lift:** MED — single campaign figures likely cherry-picked; category baseline of ~4.7% is credible.
