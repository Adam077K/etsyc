# Survey Distribution & Recruitment Plan

**Survey:** "Can shoppers tell what's AI-made?" (Etsy × Columbia challenge)
**Live link:** https://forms.gle/4eEaAVcemzkc7Www5
**Goal:** ~100+ quality responses from online shoppers, 18+.

---

## The strategy in one line

**Recruit through channels where a real person is asking real people — your own network first, then the one Reddit sub that welcomes surveys.** This gets more *and cleaner* responses than mass-posting, which is why we're doing it this way.

### Why not mass-post to Reddit / use burner accounts (decided, don't revisit)

- Reddit auto-removes new/no-karma accounts and repeated links as spam — the posts wouldn't even survive, so there's no reach to gain.
- Automated posting is against Reddit's ToS; it gets the account banned and the `forms.gle` link **blacklisted sitewide**, killing the channel for everyone on the team.
- Cold links from strangers produce trollable, low-quality data — bad for a graded test.
- A project carrying Columbia's and Etsy's names shouldn't be caught running a posting bot.

Bottom line: the honest path isn't the "nice" option, it's the **higher-yield** option for a class-sized sample.

## Rules that keep the channel alive

- Post from **real accounts with history**, never burners — no-karma accounts get auto-removed.
- **Read each subreddit's rules first;** message mods or use the weekly promo thread where required.
- **Reply to every comment** — engagement is what spreads a post, and it's what mods reward.
- **Deliver on the promise:** come back and share results. That earns goodwill for round two.
- Don't post the identical text everywhere in a short window — space it out and tailor per community.

---

## Response-quality tips

- The **"forward to 2 people"** ask in the WhatsApp version is the single biggest multiplier — keep it.
- Frame it as a **quiz / "test yourself,"** not "a survey." It's honest (it literally is a detection quiz) and it converts far better.
- Watch the first ~20 responses for junk; if trolling appears from a Reddit post, note the source so you can filter that segment in analysis.
- Aim to close recruitment once responses plateau — quality over raw N.
