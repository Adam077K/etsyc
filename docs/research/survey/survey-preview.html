The result you’re building toward

Every question maps to a piece of the proof. Here’s the headline it’s engineered to produce:

“Shoppers rated their own AI-spotting ability at 4 / 5 — but scored just 51%, no better than a coin flip. And 68% said they’d be less likely to buy once they knew.”

Core proofScore on Q5–Q8 near 50% = buyers genuinely can’t tell
The killer chartQ4 confidence vs. actual score = they think they can, but can’t
Does it matterQ9 — most say “less likely to buy” → the misperception has a cost
Transparency demandQ10 + Q11 → who should own AI disclosure
