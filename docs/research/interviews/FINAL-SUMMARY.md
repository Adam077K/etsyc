## 2. What we actually learned (the 6 that matter)

1. **The buyer's real job is "don't get scammed," not "support artisans."** Authenticity = anti-scam
   insurance. Julia bought because the stone *"was real,"* not out of craft romance. Build for
   *proof of realness*, not artisan virtue.

2. **Say/do gap is the headline finding.** 4/4 buyers exalt local/unique; one recalls **no** recent
   local purchase, another (5+ yrs NYC) barely any. High professed value, low frequency. **A business
   cannot be built on what people admire — only on what they do.**

3. **Price beats principle — on both sides, back-to-back.** The seller: *"I prefer small business, but
   sometimes the cost is expensive, so I'll go to the big business."* A buyer canceled an in-store trip
   after price-checking online. Values do not survive contact with a price tag.

4. **The human connection buyers say they value is absent by design on Etsy.** Buyers frame local as
   human (*"the guy was really nice"*); the seller's lived reality is *"almost no interaction at all with
   the customer."* Discovery happens; **relationship does not.**

5. **The seller is the higher-pain, lower-alternative user** — but overloaded and apathetic about
   effort (05: no tools/relationship; 06: *"shouldn't have to be a CMO... a CFO... a CX officer"*).

6. **"AI-made" is real but undifferentiated.** Buyers bundle AI/fake/drop-shipped/low-quality into one
   "not genuine" blob. Cannot yet claim "AI" is a distinct concern.

## 3. How this evidence bears on the locked "In the Making" bet

The bet (see `realness-goal.md`): an **un-fakeable, discovery-time, near-zero-seller-effort
proof-of-human** — the buyer watches timestamped, camera-signed moments of their item being made
(**Proof-of-Batch**: *"made in a batch we filmed; yours is #7 of 12"*). Buyer-side, in Dina Murphy's
portfolio; primitive named on slide 1 as "Identity-Bound Commerce / Human Provenance Network."

**The interviews corroborate it — hard:**

- **Learning 1 → the whole thesis.** "Proof of realness, not artisan virtue" is precisely what a
  signed make-moment delivers. The interviews say buyers buy *"real / as-advertised"*; In-the-Making is
  the un-fakeable proof of exactly that.
- **Learning 4 → why NOT a relationship product.** Connection is absent by design and the seller won't
  supply it. The bet's anti-goal ("do not build a messaging/relationship product — dies on seller
  apathy") is directly what the seller voice (05) predicts.
- **Learning 5 → why seller effort must be near-zero.** The seller is high-pain but effort-averse. This
  is the strongest interview support for D4 (zero-hands QR-sticker + shelf-phone rig) — a seller-chore
  product would die on the apathy 05 demonstrates.
- **Learning 2/3 → the crux and the honesty guards.** Say/do gap + price-defection are exactly why the
  bet refuses to "sell real as a premium at the comparison moment" (Gate 3) and states an honest
  1.4–2.2× return loop, not 3× (D9).

**The one tension (already adjudicated, logged for the record):** the interviews frame the **seller** as
the higher-pain user, which naively argues for seller-*side* tooling — and file 06 is exactly that
framing. The board resolved this deliberately: build the **buyer-side** experience (Dina's portfolio,
demoable cold), keep seller effort near-zero. The interviews don't reopen that call; they reinforce
*why* seller effort must vanish. **No change to D1–D9.**

**What files 06–07 add:** 06 is a clean verbatim articulation of the trust/transparency problem —
usable as the pitch's problem spine (C3 in the action list). 07 is delivery craft for Jul 24. Neither
is demand evidence.
