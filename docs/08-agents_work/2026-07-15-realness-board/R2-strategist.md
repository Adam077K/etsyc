# R2 — Strategist Cross-Critique

**Verdict:** PROCEED_WITH_CONDITIONS (unchanged from R1). **Ordering decided: Option A — In-the-Making hero + Trust-Graph roadmap.**

## The spine question, decided

Three of us (visionary, broad-adversary, me) gestured at competing spines in R1. I now commit against Option B (Trust-Graph hero + In-the-Making demo scene) and lock Option A on three grounds — competitive positioning, demoability, and Dina Murphy portfolio fit. Broad-adversary's durability argument is legitimate and I absorb it as a **required roadmap slide with teeth**, not as a spine swap.

### 1. Demoability — Option A wins decisively (10 min, live artifact)

- **Option A** has a **single-user emotional arc** we can literally play on stage: risk-modeler's live-recruited Columbia maker + buyer, real order Day 4, three pings play across the week, real thread rendered live. That is a **live artifact**, not a slide.
- **Option B** has a **network cold-start with n=0**. R0 explicitly names "worst cold-start" as Trust-Graph's known weakness. In 7 build days we cannot bootstrap a maker-vouches-maker graph with any density that survives a live demo — it becomes a diagram and a promise. Panels remember demos; they discount diagrams.
- The panel is 10 minutes. The math of "show, don't diagram" is brutal at that length.

### 2. Dina Murphy champion-fit — Option A is in her portfolio, Option B is not

- Dina Murphy is **VP Product Management, Buyer Experience**. Her scope is buyer-facing surfaces, buyer trust perception, and buyer retention. Her scope is not marketplace integrity, seller ops, or trust-and-safety infrastructure.
- **In-the-Making is a buyer experience product** — anticipation thread, in-app return loop, buyer-facing proof-of-human. It slots directly into her roadmap and she can walk it to her leadership as *her* buyer-side answer to Keep Commerce Human.
- **Trust-Graph is a marketplace primitive** — seller-side, integrity-side, network-side. It's a Vanessa Barnett / Marketplace Integrity pitch, not a Buyer Experience pitch. If we hero Trust-Graph, Dina hears a great idea she cannot champion internally — she'd have to hand it to a peer. **A champion who has to hand off does not champion.**
- This is the single most under-weighted variable in the R1 board. It's the reason my R1 opened on her brand-vs-product coherence gap, and it's the reason the spine has to sit in her portfolio.

### 3. Competitive positioning — Option A differentiates against the field AND absorbs the durability critique

- Every other team pitches "help Gen Z connect with makers" (romantic connection product). An emotional single-buyer anticipation arc that also names the **frequency wound** (3x/yr, −11% habitual YoY) differentiates on both emotion and business math.
- Broad-adversary's real point is that generative video decays the primitive in 12–18 months. That's the question a *smart* judge will ask. Option A answers it by making **Trust-Graph the Phase-3 slide with teeth**: identity-bound vouching is a social primitive Sora cannot fake. This converts the strongest KILL argument in the room into our own moat slide. We use their punch as our jab.
- Reverse the ordering (Option B) and the durability argument becomes a category thesis without a shippable wedge — investors and product panels both discount "network we'll build" versus "buyer moment we shipped."
