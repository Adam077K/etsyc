# R1 — Visionary Verdict: "In the Making" as the pitch spine

## Horizon-3 Vision (2028-2030)

By 2029 every consumer marketplace surface — Amazon, Etsy, TikTok Shop, Shopify's frontends — is drowning in AI-generated product images, AI-written listings, AI-voiced maker videos, and dropshipped physical objects that were "designed" by a prompt. Veo/Sora-class video is indistinguishable from a phone camera at consumer viewing distance. C2PA camera-signing exists but is shopper-illegible. The scarce, non-commoditizable resource in commerce is no longer product, taste, or logistics — it is **verified human provenance rendered as an experience**. The category winner is not a marketplace with a "real makers" filter. It is a **human-provenance network** where every object carries a live chain of hands and every maker is a subscribable feed. Etsy either becomes that network or is disintermediated by whichever platform (Whatnot, Substack-for-objects, a new entrant) builds it first. The move Etsy must make by 2028 is to stop being a store of listings and become a graph of makers — where discovery is human-first, trust propagates peer-to-peer, and the atomic unit of commerce is the moment a human made a thing.

## Contrarian Insight

The pitch team has picked the correct WEDGE and the wrong FRAME. "In the Making" is a beautiful atom — timestamped, camera-signed, un-fakeable-today, seller-effort-free, and it exploits the wait-window that only handmade commerce has. That is genuinely a new primitive: "the wait becomes the product" is not incrementally clever, it is the first shopper-legible rendering of C2PA-style provenance, and it turns Etsy's structural weakness (slow fulfillment) into a moat competitors literally cannot copy (Amazon has no wait to instrument). Keep the atom.

But the team is about to pitch it as a **feature that fixes belief at purchase**, which is the wrong wound. Etsy's real wound is frequency (3x/yr, 50% one-and-done, habitual buyers -11% YoY). In-the-Making gives you a 3x return loop *per purchase* — which multiplies a rare event and does not create a new habit. The frequency-fixing move is Follow-the-Hands: makers become subscribable feeds, "product page becomes a person" (Whatnot did $8B / 95 min per day per user on exactly this insight), and buyers open Etsy daily to watch hands, not quarterly to buy a mug. Trust-Graph is the reputation layer that keeps the feed from being gameable at scale.

The contrarian move is not to swap the spine. It is to **pitch the OS, demo the atom**. "In the Making" is the birth certificate of every object; Follow-the-Hands is the discovery feed; Trust-Graph is the reputation layer. Pitching In-the-Making alone gets a "nice feature, ships Q1" reaction from Dina Murphy. Pitching the three-layer OS with In-the-Making as the demo-able wedge gets a "this is what Keep Commerce Human means" reaction — which is the pillar the new CEO staked her tenure on.

## Why Now

The 12-18 month window is the shopper-legibility gap. C2PA camera-signing shipped at the OS level but no marketplace has rendered it in plain-English at point-of-search. Veo/Sora will close the fake-resistance of process video by mid-2028; whichever platform stakes the "human-provenance" primitive before then owns the category, because retrofitting it after fakes flood the zone is impossible (trust cannot be rebuilt post-collapse). Etsy has 24 months, not 5, to stake the flag. This pitch is the leverage point — the new CEO is publicly committed to "Keep Commerce Human" but has no product primitive to enforce the claim. We are handing her the primitive.

## Answers to the 5 questions

**1. Single strongest reason "In the Making" loses the panel or fails testing:** It reads as a well-designed feature, not a category move. Dina Murphy has seen every "help buyers connect with makers" pitch. If we present In-the-Making as a purchase-flow enhancement, the panel files it under "roadmap consideration" and picks whichever team dared to reframe the marketplace. Testing failure mode is different and more dangerous: sellers do the first clip enthusiastically and drop off by clip 3 because the "passive capture" abstraction leaks — cameras have to be positioned, the item has to be in frame, the maker has to remember. If passive capture is not actually passive, the whole spine collapses to "seller effort with extra steps," which our own discovery data killed as a dead-end.

**2. Fastest path to proving it wrong by Day 5:** Do not test the buyer side first — buyers will love a well-designed anticipation thread in a Figma clickthrough. Test the seller side. Recruit 3 real Etsy makers with pending orders, ship them the passive-capture instruction (phone mount + one-tap start), and measure: (a) how many complete 3 clips per order without a nudge, (b) time cost per clip in seconds, (c) drop-off by order number. If completion rate is under 70% by order 3 or time cost is over 30 seconds, the atom is not actually passive and the spine breaks. Run this Day 5 with a paper prototype; the buyer test can wait to Day 8.

**3. Is "In the Making" actually the right spine?** Yes as the DEMO spine, no as the PITCH spine. In-the-Making is the only one of the four that is demo-able in 7 days, testable with real makers, and legibly novel to a product panel in 90 seconds. Follow-the-Hands is the stronger long-term bet (frequency-fixing, Whatnot-validated, aligns to habit not event) but is impossible to demo credibly in a student sprint — feeds need content density that a two-week test cannot produce. Trust-Graph is the most defensible moat but has a cold-start problem that will visibly break in demo. The raw capture-atom is too small to carry a pitch. **The right move: pitch a three-layer OS (Atom → Feed → Graph), demo the atom, sketch the feed and graph as the 12-24 month roadmap.** Judges reward vision-with-a-shipped-wedge over either pure vision or pure feature.

**4. Single change that most improves win-probability:** Reframe the pitch title and opening slide from "In the Making" (a feature name) to something that names the category — "The Human Provenance Network" or "Etsy's Operating System for Real" — and put In-the-Making as slide 3, not slide 1. The rest of the pitch stays. This costs a day of narrative rework and doubles the ceiling of the panel's reaction from "cool feature" to "you just named what Kruti is trying to build."

**5. What would make me change my vote to KILL or PAUSE:** Two things. First, if the Day-5 seller test shows passive capture is not actually passive (completion <70% by order 3, or >30 seconds per clip), the atom is dead and the whole spine collapses — pivot to Trust-Graph immediately, which has the worst cold-start but does not depend on any seller behavior we cannot control. Second, if research surfaces that another HLV team is pitching a Follow-the-Hands-style feed, we lose the vision high ground by staying atom-only and must escalate our own frame to the OS pitch or forfeit. Absent those, PROCEED.

## Risk if we don't take the contrarian frame

If we ship In-the-Making as a standalone feature pitch, we lose the panel to whichever team pitches the bigger frame (probably a Follow-the-Hands or trust-network variant, because those are the obvious contrarian moves any decent strategist arrives at). We also lose it to Whatnot in the 24-month market — they already have the feed, the dwell time, and the creator-trust primitive; the only thing they lack is the handmade-provenance atom, which is exactly what we are handing them if we publish In-the-Making without also staking the OS-level frame.
