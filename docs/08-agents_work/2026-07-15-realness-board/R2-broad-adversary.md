# R2 — Broad-Adversary (cross-critique)

**R1 verdict:** PAUSE. Move Trust-Graph to spine; In-the-Making = demo scene 1.
**R2 verdict:** **PROCEED_WITH_CONDITIONS** — moved, but only if the six conditions below all bind. Otherwise revert to PAUSE.

The board did not just pattern-match against my thesis; three peers produced genuinely new artifacts I did not have in R1. I concede where they defuse the failure modes and hold ground where they don't. The concession is real — the remaining dissent is narrower and load-bearing.

---

## Cross-examination of the four mitigations

### (a) Risk-modeler's zero-hands rig (QR sticker + shelf-mounted phone) vs FM-1 (seller apathy)

**Partial defuse. Concede for stationary-bench crafts; hold for the rest.**

The QR-sticker + shelf-phone rig is the first concrete artifact I've seen that changes the physics of capture rather than pleading with sellers to change behavior. Sticker-on-order is a pack-out gesture (~2s, already in the workflow). Shelf phone is passive. This is materially different from Etsy Studio's ask ("edit and publish a video"). I take my FM-1 probability from ~75% down to ~50% for the slice of makers who work at a fixed bench.

But the rig assumes a bench. Real Etsy handmade includes: jewelry made at a kitchen table shared with a family, textile work that migrates room-to-room, ceramics with a wheel in one place and a kiln in another, leatherwork that moves between cutting mat and sewing machine. A shelf phone requires (i) dedicated space, (ii) a spare device or willingness to lock up one's primary phone during work, (iii) charging/data-plan discipline, (iv) trust that the pipeline won't leak footage of the maker's home. The rig solves capture-effort for the maker who most looks like a professional studio and is weakest for exactly the "cottage industry" seller Etsy's brand claim depends on.

**Concession:** the rig kills the "we can't reduce seller effort" objection FOR THE DEMO. It does not kill it as a category answer. The pitch must not overclaim.

### (b) "Identity-bound named-seller accountability" reframe vs FM-2 (Sora/Veo decay)

**Concede. This defuses FM-2 without requiring Trust-Graph as spine.**

This is a smart reframe and I have to give it up. If the primitive is "the named seller stakes their store on this being real" — not "the cryptography is unfakeable" — then generative-video improvement is orthogonal. Fake = lose your store = concrete economic penalty regardless of how good Sora gets. My R1 argument was that C2PA is shopper-illegible today and Sora catches up in 12-18mo; that argument evaporates when the primitive shifts to reputation-staking.

I note however that "identity-bound named-seller accountability" IS the Trust-Graph primitive under a different name — vouching-with-your-reputation is literally what Trust-Graph does at scale. So peers have adopted my primitive while keeping my label off it. I'll take the win on substance and drop the demand for the label.

**Concession:** FM-2 is materially defused. Probability on the durability-collapse scenario drops from ~65% to ~30%, contingent on the pitch actually leading with the accountability frame and not the video-provenance frame.

### (c) Customer-voice's Proof-of-Batch reframe vs FM-4 (MTO scope)

**Partial defuse. Scope expands from ~15% to ~35% of Etsy sellers. Still not "all of Etsy."**

Proof-of-Batch (batch clip + per-item tag at pack) pulls in candles, soap, small-batch textiles, printed goods, most of the "handmade that produces in runs" slice. That's roughly 2-3x the addressable seller base of pure per-order MTO. Genuinely bigger. I concede the reframe.

What it does not fix: ready-to-ship inventory (biggest chunk of Etsy handmade), digital downloads, POD, vintage, and — ironically — the custom-commission slice where "made just after you ordered" is literally true but arrives 4-8 weeks later and thus can't sustain a 3x return loop (customer-voice already downgraded to 1.4-2.2x). Team can pitch this as "hero for the productive handmade slice, adjacent primitive for inventory (before-shot / restock-in-progress)." That's an honest story. What the team cannot honestly say is "In the Making is Etsy's answer for its whole marketplace."

**Concession:** FM-4 shrinks from a spine-breaker to a scoping condition. Panel will still ask "what about the 60% not in your slice?" — team needs a rehearsed answer that doesn't handwave.

### (d) Three-rung ladder + In-the-Making as hero + Trust-Graph on roadmap

**This is the crux. I move — but only under Visionary's category framing.**

My R1 said Trust-Graph must be the spine. Visionary reframed the question: the CATEGORY is "Human Provenance Network," the DEMO WEDGE is In-the-Making, the HORIZON is Trust-Graph. Under that framing, Trust-Graph IS the strategic spine (of the category, and of the pitch's answer to "what's the moat in 2028?"), while In-the-Making is the wedge Adam can actually build and demo in 7 days. That is not a defeat of my thesis; it's a synthesis of it with the reality that Trust-Graph has a cold-start problem the team cannot solve in a week.

I still hold the following: if the pitch is framed as "In the Making is our product" (feature-thinking) rather than "Human Provenance Network is our category and In the Making is where you enter it" (category-thinking), the Sora question kills it and the MTO-scope question kills it. Visionary's frame is load-bearing. Strategist's opening-on-Dina-Murphy's-coherence-gap is compatible.

**Concession:** In-the-Making can be the demo hero IF Trust-Graph is the strategic center-of-gravity and the pitch says so on slide 1, not slide 12.

## What I still hold (remaining dissent)

1. **Reversibility asymmetry.** If Day-5 seller test fails, pivoting to Trust-Graph with 2 days left is impossible. The Day-4 maker-consent probe (my R1 condition) MUST be a real gate with a pre-committed pivot trigger in writing. Risk-modeler proposed this independently — treat it as a hard gate, not aspirational.
2. **Rig-scope honesty.** The zero-hands rig works for stationary-bench crafts; panel will probe the edge. Pitch needs a one-sentence honest answer, not overclaim.
3. **Category claim discipline.** The pitch may claim "hero wedge for productive handmade + roadmap to whole marketplace via Trust-Graph." It may NOT claim "In the Making is the answer for Etsy." Watch for this in rehearsals.

---

## Updated conditions (six binding; failure of any → revert to PAUSE)

1. **Adopt Visionary's "Human Provenance Network" CATEGORY framing** — Trust-Graph is the strategic spine of the pitch even though In-the-Making is the demo wedge. Slide 1, not slide 12.
2. **Build and test Risk-modeler's zero-hands rig by Day 4-5** — physical QR stickers + shelf-mounted phone with a real bench-craft maker. Not aspirational; a working artifact.
3. **Lead durability answer with "identity-bound named-seller accountability"** — not C2PA, not "unfakeable video." Sora question has a one-sentence answer that stays true in 2028.
4. **Adopt Customer-voice's Proof-of-Batch scope expansion** — batch-clip + per-item tag included from Day 1; hands-only default, face opt-in; return-loop honesty (1.4-2.2x, not 3x).
5. **Day-4 maker-consent probe with pre-committed pivot trigger in writing** — cold-DM ≥25 real makers, ≥60% unassisted yes without >$5 incentive. Adam signs the pivot trigger BEFORE the probe runs, not after.
6. **Rehearse the "what about the 60% not in your slice?" question and the "what stops Sora in 2028?" question** — both must have honest one-sentence answers by Day 6.
