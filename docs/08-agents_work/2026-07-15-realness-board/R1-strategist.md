# R1 — Strategist Verdict: "In the Making"

**Persona:** Strategist
**Verdict:** PROCEED_WITH_CONDITIONS
**Timeline reframe:** This is a 10-day pitch bet, not a 4-12 week ship. Metrics are pitch-day artifacts + a day-5 kill test, not 30-day product metrics.

---

## Execution Plan (10-day window)

- **Days 3-4 — Concept lock + ladder narrative (product designer + writer):** Finalize the pitch spine as a three-rung ladder: In the Making (hero, demoed) → Follow the Hands (feed, storyboarded) → Trust Graph (defensibility, one-slide). Ladder framing is what makes a boutique feature look like a category move to Dina Murphy. Ship the pitch outline before any prototype code.
- **Days 4-5 — Kill test on the two load-bearing claims (research lead + 1 field recruiter):** (a) Recruit 5 real Etsy made-to-order sellers, ask them to capture 3 clips for their next 3 orders with a stub app; measure completion rate. (b) Show 10 Gen Z buyers a Figma-mocked anticipation thread + control listing; measure declared return-intent lift and willingness-to-pay stability under a 20% price gap. Either result under threshold triggers spine change.
- **Days 5-8 — Prototype the hero moment (frontend + product designer):** Build the buyer-side anticipation thread as a fake-back Figma-prototype-or-Expo flow with 3 real captured clips from a real maker. No seller-side app required for demo — the seller flow can be storyboarded. Hero moment = the notification landing + the third clip resolving into "your piece is ready."
- **Days 8-9 — Pitch dress rehearsal + Etsy-panel-fit polish (all):** Rehearse against Dina Murphy's known priorities: Keep Commerce Human coherence gap, frequency wound, Celebrate Being Human marketing claim the marketplace cannot enforce. Cut every slide that doesn't map to one of those three.
- **Day 10 — Deliver the pitch.**

## Success Metrics

- **Panel-vote proxy: does the concept survive the two hardest questions Dina's team will ask?** Baseline unknown — target "yes on both" from a mock panel of 3 Etsy-adjacent operators run at end of day 8. Measured via structured debrief. The two questions are: "Does this scale beyond made-to-order?" and "How does this stop the 50% one-and-done churn?" If we can't answer both crisply, the pitch loses.
- **Seller-effort claim empirically supported: ≥60% clip-capture completion across 3 orders per seller, n=5 real MTO sellers.** Baseline unknown — measure day 4-5 via WhatsApp/manual capture protocol. Below 60% means the "near-zero seller effort" claim is a lie and the pitch collapses on cross-examination.
- **Buyer preference lift under price pressure: In-the-Making listing chosen ≥40% of the time when priced 20% above a comparable no-anticipation listing, n=10 Gen Z.** Baseline unknown — measure day 5 via forced-choice A/B mock. Below 40% means the say-do gap swallows the concept exactly as the R0 grounding warned.

## Answers to the five persona questions

**Q1 — Single strongest reason "In the Making" loses the panel or fails in testing.**
The MTO-scope trap. If a panelist says "but only a fraction of Etsy GMV is truly made-to-order — most listings ship from inventory," and we don't have a crisp answer, the pitch reads as a boutique feature dressed up as a strategy. The mitigation is to lead the pitch with the ladder: In the Making is the *wedge* — the smallest surface where proof-of-human is un-fakeable — and Follow-the-Hands is the *scale play* that inherits its trust primitive to ready-to-ship inventory too. Second-strongest failure mode: the "near-zero seller effort" claim is unproven and empirically likely to be false at scale (three moments-of-capture per order is three chances to forget). If the day-5 seller test shows <60% completion, the whole spine is a lie.

**Q2 — Fastest path to proving the concept wrong by day 5.**
Run two tests in parallel on day 4-5. (a) *Seller effort test:* recruit 5 real Etsy MTO sellers via Reddit/Etsy forums, DM them a simple protocol ("shoot me a start / mid / done clip for your next 3 orders, I'll pay $50"), measure completion rate and time-per-clip. Under 60% completion or over 90 seconds per clip = kill. (b) *Buyer preference test:* mock two listings in Figma, one with an "In the Making" thread teaser, one without, priced 20% apart; forced-choice with 10 Gen Z buyers. Under 40% choosing the anticipation listing at the higher price = kill. Either failure means the spine changes on day 6 — either to Follow-the-Hands (accepting the demo pain) or to a reframed anticipation-optional atom.

**Q3 — Is "In the Making" the right spine, or is Follow-the-Hands / Trust Graph / capture-atom stronger?**
For the 10-day pitch bet: In the Making is the right spine, but only if pitched as rung one of a three-rung ladder. Follow-the-Hands is Etsy's actual endgame (feed = habit = frequency), but it's undemoable in 10 days without a critical mass of content and it will look like a fake TikTok clone in a panel demo. Trust Graph is the most defensible long-term moat but has an unsolvable cold-start problem inside the pitch window. Capture-atom alone reads as a feature request, not a strategic bet, and will lose to teams pitching bigger narratives. In the Making is the only rung that (i) demos as a single vertical moment we can prototype, (ii) survives Gen Z say-do skepticism because the buyer already committed at purchase, and (iii) inherits its power to the other two rungs when you draw the ladder.

**Q4 — Single change that most improves win-probability.**
Rewrite the pitch's opening 90 seconds around Dina Murphy's political problem, not around the buyer's emotional journey. Dina's team is currently spending Celebrate-Being-Human marketing dollars against a claim the marketplace cannot enforce, and she needs product artifacts that let her defend that spend in an internal review. Open the pitch with: "Etsy's 2026 brand promise is un-backed by product. Here's the first product moment that makes the brand claim true." That single reframe turns the pitch from "cute Gen Z feature" into "the coherence artifact your VP needs to defend her marketing budget," and Dina becomes an internal champion instead of a polite listener. Every other team will lead with the buyer. We lead with the sponsor's own strategic problem.

**Q5 — What would change my vote to KILL or PAUSE.**
Four conditions. (1) Day-5 seller test shows <60% clip completion on 3 orders — the effort claim is dead and Follow-the-Hands becomes the only honest spine. (2) External data source shows made-to-order is <25% of Etsy GMV *and* we can't credibly frame In the Making as a wedge to ready-to-ship inventory — TAM collapses. (3) Another student team announces process-video as their spine before day 6 — differentiation collapses and we need to pivot to Trust Graph framing or a wait-as-product novelty angle we own alone. (4) The Etsy panel roster changes and Dina Murphy is replaced by someone from Trust & Safety or Growth — the coherence-artifact framing loses its internal champion and the pitch needs re-anchoring around whichever wound the new panelist owns.
