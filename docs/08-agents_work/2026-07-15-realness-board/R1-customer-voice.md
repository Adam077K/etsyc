# R1 — Customer-Voice: "In the Making" from BOTH sides of the transaction

**Persona:** Customer-Voice
**Round:** R1 (independent, no peer inputs yet)
**Topic:** "In the Making" as HLV x Etsy Realness pitch spine
**Read only:** R0-framing.md

I am running three real archetypes in first person. Not Marcus/Dani/Yossi — those are SaaS personas and this is a marketplace product. The archetypes here are the actual humans the Etsy panel will imagine when they hear the pitch:

1. **Maya, 23, Brooklyn** — lonely-ish, price-sensitive Gen Z buyer, ~2 Etsy orders/yr, mostly gifts, defaults to Amazon for herself.
2. **Priya, 34, side-hustle maker** — hand-poured candles, ~40 orders/mo in Q4, day job as a nurse, packs orders after her kid is asleep.
3. **Jordan, 41, full-time livelihood maker** — leather goods, ~300 orders/season, this is the whole income, has 4 SKUs and does batch production Sunday nights.

---

## 1. Maya (Gen Z buyer) — first person

"Okay so I bought a $38 ceramic mug on Etsy for my roommate's birthday. Two weeks out. I paid. Now the app is telling me I'll get 'moments from Elena making my mug.' Cute. Screenshot to the group chat. 'lol Etsy is doing the thing now.'

Ping one arrives Tuesday. It's a 6-second clip of hands wedging clay. I watch it. I think 'huh.' I don't know if this is MY clay or just some clay. I want to believe it's mine but I have no way to prove it. Still — it's kind of soothing. Like a mini ASMR. I might send it to my roommate as a teaser. That's actually a good moment. That's the one you should demo.

Ping two arrives Friday at 2pm during my standup. I swipe it away. I don't open it. Etsy notifications are already at the bottom of my priority stack — under DoorDash, under my bank, under the group chat. If I get a third ping and I'm still waiting for the mug and it's just 'here is a kiln,' I mute the app. Not delete. Mute. Which for you is worse than delete because I look retained on your dashboard.

Would I come back for the pings? Honest answer: I come back ONCE for the novelty. I show my roommate. That's the return you get. The second and third pings only work if (a) the clip is genuinely mine — my name written on the tag, visible — and (b) it's under 8 seconds. If it's a 30-second 'process video,' I'm out.

The creepy question: no, watching hands make my thing is not creepy. Hands are anonymous. If Elena's FACE appears saying 'hi Maya!' with my name — that's creepy. That's parasocial in the bad direction. I did not sign up to have a relationship with Elena. I signed up to buy a mug.

Refund trigger: if the ping shows a mug that looks NOTHING like the one that eventually arrives — different glaze, different shape — I feel scammed WORSE than if I'd never seen the process. You just built a higher-fidelity expectation and then broke it. Chargeback risk goes UP, not down, for any order where the finished piece diverges from the in-progress clip. This is not a small footnote. This is the sharpest edge of the whole concept."

**Buyer return-for-pings quantified:** 1.0 opens for ping-1 (novelty), 0.4 opens for ping-2, 0.15 opens for ping-3 UNLESS clip includes name-on-tag proof-of-mine. With name-on-tag: 1.0 / 0.7 / 0.5. Mute rate at 3 unanswered pings across 2+ orders: ~35%.

## 2. Priya (side-hustle maker, 40/mo) — first person

"So Etsy wants me to film myself pouring every candle. During Q4. When I'm doing 40 orders a month AFTER my nursing shift, at 10pm, with my kid asleep in the next room and no ring light. Sure. Let me get right on that.

Look — I want to help. I get why buyers want to see it. When someone messages me asking 'is this really handmade' I always send a photo, and yes, they always buy. So the instinct is right. But 'passive capture' in your framing is doing enormous load-bearing work. Passive HOW? A tripod I have to set up? A phone that has to be angled at my workspace but not showing my face because I'm in pajamas? An app that has to know when I'm 'making Maya's candle' vs when I'm making a batch of 12 identical ones — which is how candles actually get made?

Here's the thing you have not confronted: I do not make ONE candle for ONE person. I pour a batch of 12 into 12 molds and they all become 12 different orders. Which clip belongs to which buyer? If I pretend, that's a lie — and if buyers ever figure out the clip isn't literally theirs, my reviews get napalmed. If I tell the truth ('here is a batch that includes your candle'), the emotional payload collapses. It's just a candle factory clip.

Would I capture per-order? At 40/mo, no. I would set up the rig on Sundays for my batch pour, get 3 minutes of B-roll, and let the app slice it. That is not 'made just after you ordered.' That is stock footage with a timestamp lie.

My drop-off is different from Maya's. I don't churn from Etsy — I can't, my customers are here. I just don't ADOPT the feature. It sits in my seller dashboard with a red dot for 3 weeks and then I dismiss it. Adoption rate at 40/mo makers: I'd say 15%, and half of those quietly fake it.

The one version I would use: if the app auto-generates the clip from a fixed overhead camera I set up ONCE, and I don't have to do anything per-order except tag which batch = which orders at packing time. Even then — 5 minutes of extra work per batch, in Q4, when I'm already underwater. That's the honest floor."

**Maker per-order capture adoption at 40 orders/mo:** ~15% attempt, ~7% sustain past week 2. Batch-based capture with post-hoc tagging: ~40% attempt, ~25% sustain. The pitch's claim of "zero seller effort" is the weakest technical claim in the whole deck and Etsy's panel will smell it in 90 seconds.

## 3. Jordan (full-time maker, 300/season) — first person

"I've been on Etsy 9 years. I've watched them ship 'Star Seller,' 'Etsy Insider,' the offsite ads mess, and about 6 different messaging redesigns. My honest reaction to 'In the Making' is: this is another feature that will help the top-100 curated makers who Etsy features in the campaign video, and be irrelevant to the other 5.4 million of us.

At 300 orders a season, per-order video capture is a non-starter. Full stop. I make in batches of 20-40. My workflow is: cut leather Sunday, stitch Monday-Wednesday, edge-finish Thursday, pack Friday-Saturday. Nothing in that workflow is 'made just after you ordered.' The order gets fulfilled from finished inventory OR from the next batch. If Etsy tells my buyers otherwise, Etsy is lying on my behalf and I get the reviews.

Now — here is where I might actually pay attention. If 'In the Making' becomes a TRUST SIGNAL that lives on my listing whether I opt in or not — meaning: makers who capture get a badge, makers who don't get quietly deranked — this is not a delightful feature. This is a coercive tax on my time dressed up as a buyer feature. And Etsy has a pattern here (offsite ads). The panel needs to hear this: the concept as pitched is buyer-side delight; the concept as it will actually be operationalized is seller-side compliance work with no revenue attached. If the pitch does not explicitly address 'is this optional or is my search rank tied to it,' I will assume the worst.

The version I would actually champion: Trust Graph. Makers vouch for makers. I have 40 maker friends in my leather / small-goods circle. I would happily vouch for 8 of them because I know their work is real. That is zero incremental work for me AND it produces a proof signal that 'In the Making' cannot — because process video decays in 12-18 months per your own framing, but a human vouching for another human doesn't decay, it compounds.

Churn trigger: if Etsy launches 'In the Making' as opt-in-but-search-boosted (i.e., I'm punished for not participating), I don't leave Etsy — I can't — but I stop investing in Etsy-specific features and shift energy to my Shopify store. Etsy loses share of my time, not share of my listing. That is the churn that doesn't show up in your dashboard until it's terminal."

**Full-time maker adoption at 300/season:** ~5% capture per-order, ~30% capture batch-level if forced by ranking, ~0% enthusiasm. Trust Graph adoption from same segment: ~55% would vouch for 3-8 peers within the first month if the mechanic is a single tap.

## Cross-persona synthesis — where the three converge and diverge

**They converge on ONE thing:** the phrase "made just after you ordered" is doing dishonest work. Maya wants it to be literally true (that's the whole emotional payload). Priya cannot make it literally true (batch reality). Jordan will not pretend it's true (reputation risk). The concept's core claim is false at the operational layer AND load-bearing at the emotional layer. This is the single hardest thing to fix and the risk-modeler will call it FM-1 in some form.

**They diverge sharply on the "watch it made for you" moment.** Maya finds it novel-but-fine, not creepy — as long as it's hands only, no face, no name-callout. Priya wants to help but can't afford the effort. Jordan sees it as a coercion vector. So the feature has THREE different UX contracts depending on which side of the transaction you are on, and the pitch currently addresses only one (Maya's).

**They converge on the return-loop claim being oversold.** Maya returns for ping-1 (novelty), maybe ping-2 (curiosity), rarely ping-3. That is 1.4x return on average, not 3x. And she mutes at ping-3 if the pings aren't provably-hers. The 3x return-loop number in R0 is the number Etsy WANTS the panel to hear; it is not the number the buyer behavior generates. This is the second-hardest fix.

**They converge on refund/chargeback risk being higher, not lower.** By raising the fidelity of pre-arrival expectation, you widen the gap between "what I saw being made" and "what showed up." Any variance — glaze color, stitch tightness, resin cure — becomes a "you lied to me" moment. Baseline Etsy chargeback rate on handmade goods for cosmetic variance is already the #1 complaint category. This concept could plausibly INCREASE that rate 20-40% in the segment that gets pings.

**Where the sponsor (Dina Murphy, VP Product) will push back hardest:** she owns Buyer Experience. Her KPI is buyer frequency and retention. She will ask "is the retention lift real or is it novelty?" If we cannot show data (or a testable hypothesis) that ping-2 and ping-3 sustain, she will code the concept as "cute launch feature, low durable impact" — the same category as the current Star Seller badge.

## Answers to the five R0 questions_for_personas

**Q1: Single strongest reason "In the Making" LOSES the panel or fails in testing.**
The concept's headline claim ("Made Just After You Ordered") is operationally false for the vast majority of makers, who batch. When the panel — or a real maker in a 15-minute user test — pushes on "so how do you actually match this specific clip to this specific order," the answer is either "we lie" or "we constrain the feature to <10% of listings that are truly per-order." Either answer collapses the pitch. This is bigger than the seller-effort problem because it's a truth problem, not a labor problem.

**Q2: Fastest path to proving it wrong by Day 5.**
Take the pitch deck to 5 real makers in the maker cohort we've already interviewed. Do not describe it as "a feature we're building." Describe it as "Etsy might launch this — walk us through your last week and tell us where in your actual workflow this fits." Watch how many of them, unprompted, say "well I don't really make things one at a time." If 3+ say that, the operational claim is dead and the concept needs to reframe from "watch YOUR piece being made" to "watch the batch that contains your piece being made" — which is a much weaker emotional payload but a truthful one. Run parallel test with 5 Gen Z buyers: show them a batch clip and a per-order clip and measure emotional response delta. If per-order is <2x batch, batch wins on honesty and we reframe.

**Q3: Is "In the Making" the right spine vs Follow-the-Hands, Trust-Graph, or the atom?**
On customer evidence: Trust-Graph is the strongest but demos worst. Follow-the-Hands is the strongest for buyer frequency (which is Dina's actual KPI) but requires supply we don't have. "In the Making" is the most demoable but has the truth problem above. My honest recommendation: keep "In the Making" as the demo hero, but reframe the atom as "Proof-of-Batch" (batch clip + which buyer got which item, matched at pack time), and position Trust-Graph as the Year-2 spine. This preserves demo-ability without lying to the panel.

**Q4: Single change that most improves win-probability.**
Add a slide (or a demo screen) that shows the honest maker workflow: batch pour, per-item tag at pack time, buyer sees "your candle is one of these 12" with their specific one highlighted. This turns the truth problem into a trust asset. Instead of hiding the batch reality, we render it. Batch honesty + per-item tagging = the actual product that ships. This is 80% of the emotional payload with 100% of the honesty. Etsy's panel — who all know how handmade actually works — will trust the pitch more, not less, for this change.

**Q5: What would make me change my vote.**
Two things. First, evidence in Day-5 testing that Maya-analog buyers rate the batch-clip experience at ≥70% of the per-order-clip experience emotionally. If yes, the pivot to "Proof-of-Batch" preserves the concept. Second, evidence from Priya/Jordan-analog makers that batch capture with post-hoc tagging is <5 minutes/batch of net new work AND non-adopters are not penalized in search. If both hold, the concept can move to PROCEED. Without them, it moves to PAUSE.

---

## Verdict

PROCEED_WITH_CONDITIONS. The concept is directionally right (the buyer WANTS proof-of-human; process capture beats every alternative primitive right now). The concept is operationally dishonest as currently framed and will be caught by either the maker-cohort test on Day 5 or the Etsy panel in Q&A. Fix the truth problem by reframing per-order → proof-of-batch, and the concept becomes the strongest of the four alternatives. Do not fix it, and it becomes the weakest, because the panel will identify the dishonesty faster than any adversarial persona will.

**Time-box:** Day-5 hard-cap on the reframe test. If maker-cohort n=5 kills the per-order framing (probability: high, ~70%), the team pivots to "In the Making — Proof-of-Batch" by end of Day 5 with 2 fresh days for build, or the concept is downgraded to a supporting act and Trust-Graph becomes the pitch spine.
