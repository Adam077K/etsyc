## The challenge (from the sponsor)
- Verbatim: **"How will Gen Z find, believe in, and connect with the real people behind the things they love?"**
- Sponsor: **Dina Murphy, VP Product, *Buyer* Experience, Etsy.** (Not seller tooling, not fraud ops — the buyer's felt experience of *believing*.)
- Brief warns: AI can fake listings, faces, stories; **photos/reviews/bios are dead trust signals.**
- **Q4: realness should form "instead of relying on a platform to declare it."** (Platform-issued trust badges are pre-rejected.)
- **Q5: make the un-fakeable — time, skill, care, presence — *felt*, not claimed.**
- They planted a **Gen Z loneliness report** → a strong hint toward *connection-as-product*.
- They asked for "an experience, a tool, a ritual, or a community practice — not necessarily an app," and "make something you'd use yourself."

## Our own research (directional, n=4 buyers + 1 seller, NYC)
- Buyers punish "fake" hard, but the **say-do gap is the headline**: they exalt local/unique, rarely buy it.
- **Price beats principle** on both sides; a buyer canceled an in-store trip after price-checking online.
- **Sellers are apathetic**: "almost no interaction at all with the customer," zero marketing by design, won't be a CMO; defect to big-box on price.
- **"Authenticity" ≈ "not a scam"** more than "artisan virtue."

## THE IDEA (current — this is the subject to challenge)
**A new way to shop, built around the HUMAN behind the product — not a grid of comparisons.**
- You **scroll real makers telling their story** (video). You **tap in**; their **voice keeps playing** beside a **store personalized to their brand** (their colors, type, voice, atmosphere). You meet the person, then buy from them.
- **Seed:** the maker records a **few-minute interview** (voice/video) about the business, product, values, process, community. That's the raw material.
- **Co-creation:** a back-and-forth turns that into a real, quality, personalized store — **AI does the design/story/marketing work the maker can't** (they're an artist, not a web designer). *Framing guardrail: never "AI does it for you" — it's a process WITH the maker; they stay the creative author.*
- **Trust anchor:** the **real human is always present** (raw interview plays throughout) **+ AI is bounded by design** — it can never fake the person or distort the product's truth. AI flips from the buyer's #1 fear to the thing that makes trust possible.
- **Price:** **connection earns a premium**, paid because **connection lands BEFORE price** (no grid → no side-by-side to defect on).
- **Positioning:** its own app / new way of shopping, **Etsy-compatible as an add-on**; universal ambition (vision slide carries the moonshot).

Founder's own thesis, verbatim (from a recording — this is the *voice*): *"Everything can be faked now. AI is used to make fake things — to steer what you buy, to get your money. So human connection becomes the thing people are actually looking for. Give a buyer a real connection to the maker, and trust — and he'll want to support them. Even pay a little more, if it comes to that."* And: makers are *"still passionate… they shouldn't have to be a CMO at the same time as a CFO, at the same time a CX officer… there's no transparency… you take the slack for the seller."*
