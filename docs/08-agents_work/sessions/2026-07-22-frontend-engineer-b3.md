## Scrim architecture change — stated plainly for the QA review (routed by team-lead)
WHAT CHANGED: the over-film text backdrop moved off the frame-wide `.kol-scrim::after` gradient (fixed geometry: solid at bottom, transparent at 55% of FRAME height) onto the chrome band itself. `.kol-hero-chrome` — the absolutely-positioned bottom overlay holding h1+caption — now paints its own background: opaque `--scrim-strong` (new theme var, `ground 40% oklab→black`, emitted by both theme paths + root fallback) under its content box, fading to transparent across a reserved `padding-top` zone (`min(128px,12cqw)`) that no text may enter. `.kol-scrim::after` (the `--scrim` frame veil) is UNCHANGED and still paints beneath. WHY: the 55%-of-frame gradient's coverage was fixed while the text band's height was content-dependent — a 3-line statement rose past the covered band onto raw film (the 1.04:1 P1), and even inside the band the gradient's partial alpha composited with footage (the 3.78:1 caption). Because the band is the text's own container, coverage tracks content by construction, and text contrast becomes `contrast(--on-media, --scrim-strong)` — footage-independent, which is what let the ≥5.5 floor be asserted in unit tests (`scrim-strong.test.ts`) instead of re-measured per capture. Companion mechanism: `fitStatementScale` (approved as canonical) bounds the band to ≤0.8 of the frame so the film always wins. RISK SURFACE for review: `.kol-hero-chrome` CSS (globals.css), `--scrim-strong` emissions (curated.ts/custom.ts), the fit hook in hero-video/index.tsx, and the assumption that no OTHER over-media text relies on the old 55% band (kol-scrim's other consumer is FilmFrame chrome; reviewed uses in hero only).
