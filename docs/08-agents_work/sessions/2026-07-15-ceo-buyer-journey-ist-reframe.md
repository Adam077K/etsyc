## The IST we went deep on
**"You can't build a relationship through generic search."** Google / Etsy / Amazon match a query to a listing and the session ends. The platform keeps the customer on purpose; the maker gets a stranger every time. Delight never compounds into loyalty because there's no thread back.

## Key calls / decisions
- **The three goals are secretly enemies.** Adam's goal-reframe — (1) customer finds what they want, (2) business connects with the customer, (3) customer stays on the platform — only conflicts while the relationship lives *off*-platform. Platforms retain customers *by* stopping makers from owning them. **Move the relationship on-platform and (2) and (3) become the same move.** This is the unlock, and it satisfies the Day 5–6 "value to buyer, seller AND Etsy" requirement natively.
- **The whitespace is real.** Shopify owns the relationship but has no discovery; Etsy/Amazon own discovery but block the relationship; TikTok/IG do relationship-first discovery. **Nobody owns "discovery that becomes relationship."**
- **Etsy is the paradox incumbent** — brand is human/maker/story, mechanics are anonymous search + platform-owned customers. Best-placed to flip it *and* the biggest structural blocker. Repeat buyers grow Etsy GMV, so an on-platform relationship layer is aligned with Etsy, not against it.
- **The opening (what to build):** not better search — the missing relationship layer, where *finding a maker becomes following a maker* and the thread lives on-platform.
