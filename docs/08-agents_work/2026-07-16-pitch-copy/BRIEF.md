## The product (locked — do not change the idea, only the words)
**KOL** — Hebrew for **"voice."** Double meaning we lean on: the maker's literal *voice* + their voice finally being *heard*.
A **new way to shop, built around the real human behind the product — not a grid.** You scroll the *making* (real hands, real things) with the maker's own **voice** as the soundtrack; you tap in and their voice keeps playing beside a store that feels like them; you meet the person and buy from them. The maker records one short **interview** (a few minutes); the tech does the design/story work they can't — but **never speaks for them; they stay the creative author.** Trust anchor: **her own voice, cited to timecode — "tap any line, hear her say it."** (NOT "AI bounded by design" — that's dead.) Price: lead with **support**; "even pay a little more, if it comes to that" is the honest tail (no number). Positioning: its own app, Etsy-compatible. Vision: **grounded** ("a new way to shop from real humans") — no moonshot jargon.

## TWO fresh constraints from the founder (must honor)
1. **Not everything online is human-made.** Some products are machine-made, mass-produced, drop-shipped. Do NOT claim "there's a person behind everything you buy." The honest, stronger frame: *some* things are made by real people and some by machines, and **online you can't tell which anymore** — KOL is how you find and connect with the real ones. (This is also exactly the brief's "find the real people.")
2. **Slide 5 is about VOICE → TRUST, not "meet the maker before the product."** Center it on *giving the brand's/maker's voice a way to be heard* and *building trust/connection* with the buyer — the meaning of "Kol." Avoid the transactional "meet before you buy" framing.

## Voice & delivery (non-negotiable)
- **Plain, human, unpolished** — like the founder's own recorded words: *"they shouldn't have to be a CMO at the same time as a CFO, at the same time a CX officer… take the slack for the seller."* No ad-copy slickness, no jargon, no hype.
- **Let them discover it** (pitch-coaching): don't over-explain; frame it so the room *arrives* at the insight and leans in.
- Warmth about the makers as the **foundation** of shopping; we want to *hear their stories*.
- **Mutual enablement:** the maker (never a designer/marketer) is *empowered* to express their creativity/beliefs via the tech — that's the engine of the buyer seeing the real human. Both sides get their humanity back.
