# HLV Mentor Feedback — the pitch structure he wants
*Source: `New_Recording_46_eng.pdf` (6-min conversation with a senior HLV person, 2026-07-23). Speaker 1 = the mentor walking the ideal deck. HIS IDEAS OVERRIDE our prior structure where they conflict (Founder directive). Integrate with — don't discard — our accuracy rails + sourced numbers + craft.*

## The single biggest shift
**STORY becomes the spine of the whole pitch — not trust/verification.** His framing:
- *"Introducing [KOL] — e-commerce brought into the day and age of storytelling."*
- Three main things: (1) **behind every object is a story** — why it exists, how it's made; (2) humans have told stories for thousands of years, yet this **disappears** when the objects are made by humans — we're changing that; (3) enabling a new kind of experience: **scripted, story-based shopping** — by building the experience that matches the *stories* behind the items.
- The load-bearing line: *"It starts by **rebuilding the e-commerce experience around the storytelling elements** — the brand, the story behind items, the materials, the purpose/why. By putting these up front… **the foundation is the story itself, not the products.**"*
Trust/connection stays — but it's the *result* of the story, not the headline.

## The narrative he wants (in order)
1. **Intro / what it is** — "e-commerce for the age of storytelling" + the three main things (above).
2. **The maker, first** — Sharon, a real maker with a store. She signs into [KOL] and expresses the story behind her work. **Walk the SELLER journey** (she's already invested in communicating her store, but it lacks the real story behind it; [KOL] lets her tell it).
3. **Then a named BUYER** — *"introduce a new person."* **Noy — a new dad**, wants something with **personality / custom for his child**, not fast fashion. He's shopping, doesn't know where, **comes across her store, connects with it, feels the story, buys two.** Gets them home: quality is high, feels like expression, and **he can tell the story forward.** → *"Creating better lifetime value for all her customers."*
4. **How it works** — Discovery page → click/search → click a video → dive into the store → see the product → click → shop. **Keep it basic, no fluff.** Then: **connection and trust.**
5. **Data / reasons why** — the research findings ("we found that … is why this is necessary").
6. **Market** — *"e-commerce is what it is, but storytelling is disconnected — it lives in social networks / content platforms, not the shopping experience. Some tried (Instagram) and failed."* By TikTok/Instagram trying it, we see it's a strong element. For human-made, meaningful goods, **[KOL] stands in the top-right corner of the market.** Size: **~$10.5B opportunity**, framed as **"the missing sales that should have been there"** — differentiation + story we believe = a **20–40% price loss when you compete without a story being told.**
7. **Business model** — **three elements: platform fee + transaction fee + the tools** (services / "showings" potentially).
8. **How it's made possible (diagram)** — *"this is why it's different and how it's made possible."* Introduce **the tools** here (first mention): a **short interview + a walk of how she's enabled to express her creativity, ideas, and values through the shop.** It's in the **setup process.**
9. **What are we missing? / Signals** — *"this makes sense for Etsy as the core/foundation of the business. Not an AB test — a **side venture that can prove a fundamental change is valid or not.**"* Run it **in a niche within Etsy** where stories are rich (**sewing / clothing / handmade / glassblowing / pottery**). The signal: **A/B a normal Etsy seller page vs. a [KOL] seller page** — measure **conversion, add-to-basket** (can't fully prove in 100 days; **hypothesis = lifetime-value impact**).
10. **Vision — at the very end** — *"it may not be the future of all e-commerce, but for handmade goods and things humans stand behind, **story is what differentiates more than anything now — so story should be front and center in everything sold in the future.**"* Close on that.
