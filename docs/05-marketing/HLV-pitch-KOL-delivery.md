# KOL — Delivery Script (~3:00, rehearsal-ready)
*What you SAY, trimmed to time, with cues. Slide 74pt text is unchanged (see build sheet). [pause] = 1 beat · [—] = short breath · [slow]/[land it] = drop pace, hold eye contact.*

**Pace:** ~140 words/min. If you're rushing, you'll finish at 2:30 and it'll feel thin — let the [pause]s breathe.
**Three lines to nail (slow down, look up):** "Not just trust. Connection." · "Tap any line — hear the second she said it." · "Every maker, finally heard."

---

**[S3 · Problem]** *(calm, conversational — you're just noticing something true)*
Behind so much of what you buy, there's a person. A real maker, with a story, a craft, a reason they do this. **[pause]** The kind of story you'd love to hear. But you don't. Online, her whole world gets flattened to a photo, a price, a rating. One square in a grid of near-identical squares. Nowhere to be different, nowhere for a connection to start. **[breath]** So we scroll, we buy, and we feel nothing, from no one. **[pause]** It wasn't always like this. Walk into a real shop, and you *meet* the person who made it. They tell you the story, the little things. That is why you trust them. We just never brought that online. **[pause]** And now it's even harder. Some of what you buy is made by a person, some by a machine, and you honestly can't tell which anymore. And the real maker? She's an artist, not a marketer. She has no way to show she's real. **[land it]** So the human disappears, on both sides.

**[S4 · Introducing]** *(a small lift)*
So we built a way to find them — and actually meet them. We call it **Kol**. **[pause]** In Hebrew, that means *voice* — because a maker's voice is the thread back to the whole human behind the thing.

**[S5 · What it is]** *(the heart — slow down here)*
Kol is a new kind of marketplace. Not a grid of listings, but the maker's own world: her colors, her words, her voice running through all of it. You see her hands work. You hear why she does this. You feel the place it comes from, like standing in her actual shop. You meet the maker before you meet the product. **[pause]** Two things happen at once. You stop wondering if she's real, because you can hear her. And you start to *care*, because you've met her. **[land it]** Not just trust. Connection.

**[S6 · How it works]** *(paint it — let them see it)*
Here's what it feels like. You open Kol — no grid. There's a woman at a wheel — call her Sarah — clay turning under her hands, her voice over it, telling you about the first bowl she ever sold. You tap in, and her voice keeps going while her whole world opens around you — her colors, her studio. **[—]** Sarah never had to be a designer. She just *talked* — and the tech built the world around her, *with* her, never for her. She stays the artist. **[slow]** And every word in her store is really her. Her voice runs through all of it. Tap any line — hear the second she said it. **[pause]** You didn't compare Sarah to anyone. You met her. And you bought from her.

**[S7 · Who it helps]** *(pick the pace back up)*
That's better for everyone. The buyer meets her, feels her world, and wants to support her. The maker, who was never a marketer, finally shows who she really is — a whole brand, in her own voice and colors — and still stays the artist. And Etsy's oldest promise — real people — comes true.

**[S8 + S9 · Measure + why]** *(steady)*
We'll know it's working when buyers come back for the *person*, not the product. Because it turns shopping from a transaction back into a relationship. And the more the world fakes, the more we get to choose real.

**[S10 · How we start]** *(grounded, confident)*
We start with one. One maker, one honest recording — a few minutes of her talking. And we build her world *with* her — starting from her voice.

**[S11 · Vision]** *(warm, open)*
That's the world we want. A new way to shop from real humans — where you connect with the real people behind the things you love.

**[S12 · Close]** *(slow — land each line, then open your hand to the room)*
Kol. **[pause]** Every maker, finally heard. **[—]** If you want to help build it — come find me. Because everyone behind everything you love has a voice, a story, a world worth stepping into. **[land it]** Let's make sure they're heard. **[pause]** Thank you.
