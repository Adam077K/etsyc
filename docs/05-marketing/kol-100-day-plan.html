KOL · pilot execution plan

The first 100 days.

One story-rich category. Sharon plus a first cohort of makers. We prove the leading indicators — conversion, add-to-basket, and fewer "not as described" disputes — in 100 days, and stand up the lagging one (repeat-purchase → LTV) to be judged over the quarters after. Honest by design: trust and lifetime value take longer than 100 days, so we prove the signals now and the payoff next.
Start ~Jul 25 2026 → Day 100 ~Nov 1 2026 Wedge: one story-rich category Anchor maker: Sharon / Two Dots Model: add-on to Etsy · share of the lift

The wedge — kept deliberately small

Where
One story-rich Etsy category (Sharon's world — handmade kids' apparel)

Three phases

DAYS 0–30 · ~Aug

Prove the engine repeats.

Not hand-crafted for Sharon. It works for any maker who talks.
  • Ship the KOL UI kit — video-panel, story blocks, product shelf, cards (unblocks every mockup + the live build).
  • Second + third maker worlds live from the AI interview — the #1 conviction proof (engine repeats, voice stays theirs).
  • Interview engine to first usable state — talk → world, unpolished words preserved.
  • Verification live — Real-Maker check + verified reviews on pilot stores.
  • Instrumentation in place — capture conversion, add-to-basket, INAD/returns, repeat flags from day one.
  • Gate → Day 14 & Day 30 below
DAYS 31–60 · ~Sep

Pilot goes live.

Real buyers meet real makers in one category, measured against control.
  • 10–15 makers onboarded in the wedge category, each with a full KOL world.
  • Add-on entry live — the discovery-feed door drives buyers to pilot stores.
  • Matched control set — same makers' standard listings, so every delta is attributable.
  • Click-to-narrate shipped — the signature interaction in the real product.
  • Weekly read on conversion + add-to-basket vs. control; fix the drop-offs.
DAYS 61–100 · ~Oct

Prove the lift.

Show the leading indicators moved — and set up the quarters-long trust proof.
  • Report the deltas — conversion, add-to-basket, INAD/returns vs. control, with confidence caveats.
  • First repeat-purchase signals — early cohort returns; the LTV read is framed as ongoing.
  • Maker economics — actual incremental GMS per maker → validate/adjust the 15% lift assumption.
  • Expansion plan — second category + the makers ready to onboard next.
  • Decision pack for Etsy / next raise.

Milestones & go/no-go gates

Day 14
Engine repeats. A second, non-Sharon maker world is live from the interview and reads as authentically hers. No-go → the moat is hand-craft, not product. Fix before onboarding a cohort.
Day 30
Pilot-ready. UI kit shipped, verification live, instrumentation capturing all four metrics, 3+ maker worlds. Gate to open the cohort.
Day 60
Signal or pivot. First conversion + add-to-basket deltas vs. control are directionally positive. Flat → diagnose (discovery? trust? UX?) before scaling spend.
Day 100
Verdict. Leading indicators reported with confidence bounds; repeat-purchase trend established; lift assumption validated or revised; expansion + funding decision.

What we'd need from Etsy (if the pilot runs inside)

  • One story-rich category and a homepage entry point for the add-on.
  • A matched control and read access to conversion / return / repeat data for the cohort.
  • A data-share window — KOL keeps the engine + verified-maker/trust data.
  • Judged on trust + repeat-purchase over 2–3 quarters, not a two-week test.

Top risks & how we de-risk

  • Engine doesn't repeat → Day-14 gate catches it before we scale.
  • Makers won't be on camera → audio-from-interview + hands-only film; onboard camera-comfortable makers first.
  • Buyers skip the video → video is opt-in; lead with trust + ship-date, film is the invitation.
  • Too little traffic to read → matched control + seeded demand so deltas are attributable at small N.
