### Slide 3 — The problem
**[SLIDE]**
```
The problem

A real maker has a story,
a craft, a whole world.

Online, it's flattened
to a photo and a price.

The person disappears.
```
**[SAY]** Behind so much of what you buy, there's a person. A real maker, with a story, a craft, a reason they do this. The kind of story you'd love to hear. But you don't. Online, her whole world gets flattened to a photo, a price, a rating: one square in a grid of near-identical squares. Nowhere to be different, nowhere for a connection to start. So we scroll, we buy, and we feel nothing, from no one. It wasn't always like this. Walk into a real shop, and you *meet* the person who made it. They tell you the story, the little things. That's why you trust them. We just never brought that online. And now it's even harder: some of what you buy is made by a person, some by a machine, and you honestly can't tell which anymore. The photo can be generated, the reviews written by no one. And the real maker? She's an artist, not a marketer. She has no way to show you she's real. So the human disappears, on both sides.

### Slide 4 — Introducing
**[SLIDE]**
```
Introducing KOL.
```
**[SAY]** So we built a way to find them, and actually meet them. We call it Kol. In Hebrew, that means *voice*. Because a maker's voice is the thread we follow back to the whole human behind the thing.

### Slide 5 — What it is
**[SLIDE]**
```
KOL is a new kind of marketplace,
where the maker's world opens up.
Her voice, her craft, her colors.
You don't just trust her. You connect with her.
```
**[SAY]** Kol is a new kind of marketplace. Not a grid of listings, but the maker's own world, built in her colors, her words, her way, with her voice running through it. You see her hands work, you hear why she does this, you feel the place it comes from, like standing in her actual shop. You meet the maker before you meet the product. Two things happen at once. You stop wondering if she's real, because you can hear her. And you start to *care*, because you've met her. That's the whole point: not just trust. Connection.

### Slide 6 — It works like this
**[SLIDE]**
```
It works like this:

· A feed of real makers, on film, not a grid.
· Tap in, and her world opens.
· Her colors, her fonts, her studio, her craft.
· Built with her, never for her. She stays the artist.
· Her voice runs through all of it.
```
**[SAY]** Here's what it feels like. You open Kol, no grid. There's a woman at a wheel, call her Sarah, clay turning under her hands, filmed her way, her voice over it, telling you about the first bowl she ever sold. You tap in, and her voice keeps going while her whole world opens up around you: her colors, her fonts, her studio, the light in it, the bowl she made. A space that feels like *hers* and nobody else's. Sarah never had to become a designer or a marketer. She just *talked*, and the technology built the world around her, with her, never for her. She stays the artist. And every word in her store is really her. Tap any line, and you hear the exact second she said it. You didn't compare Sarah to anyone. You met her. And you bought from her.
