## The Challenge (verbatim)

> **How will Gen Z find, believe in, and connect with the real people behind the things they love?**

Framed by HLV as a challenge around **meaningful commerce**:
> "How can commerce connect communities around shared values and interests?"

Etsy's founding premise, restated in the brief: *"real people making meaningful things for other people."*

---

## The Sponsor's Diagnosis (their framing of the problem)

The brief argues three things are simultaneously true:

| Claim | Sponsor's words |
|---|---|
| **Demand for realness is rising** | "The desire for real things made by real people has never been stronger." Gen Z grew up where everything can be curated, faked, or generated — the more machine-made content floods feeds, the more a handmade object or real human voice cuts through. |
| **Machine-made is everywhere** | "AI can produce beautiful listings, faces, and stories beside the genuine article." |
| **The old trust signals are dead** | "Photos, reviews, and bios are easy to imitate and harder to trust." |
| **Nobody has solved it** | "Nobody has figured out what realness should look like in the age of AI." |

**The core tension:** demand for authenticity is at an all-time high at exactly the moment the signals we use to detect it stopped working.

---

## The Five Questions Worth Asking (verbatim, numbered as in brief)

1. How might we **recognize the real person** behind something online, when photos, stories, and reviews can all be imitated?
2. How might **trust form between a buyer and a maker who will never meet** in person?
3. How might buying become a **connection between two people**, rather than a transaction with a story attached?
4. How might a **community of buyers and makers recognize realness together**, instead of relying on a platform to declare it?
5. How might the things that **can't be faked — time, skill, care, presence** — become things people can *feel* when they shop?

> **Note for us:** Q4 and Q5 are the least-crowded. Q4 explicitly rejects platform-issued trust badges (the obvious answer). Q5 is about *felt* experience, not verification. These are the two the sponsor is signalling she has NOT already seen answered.

---

## What They Told Us To Do

> "Your job is not to arrive with answers. Your job is to ask better questions."

- **Start by paying attention.** Notice how you and the people around you decide what's real online. Talk to makers, buyers, family, friends. Watch yourself shop.
- **Look for** an experience, a tool, a ritual, or a **community practice** — not necessarily an app.
- **Then build and test.**
- **"Make something that you would use yourself."**

## CEO Read — What This Brief Is Actually Asking For

*(Our interpretation. Separated from the source above on purpose.)*

1. **They pre-rejected the obvious answer.** Question 4 says *"instead of relying on a platform to declare it."* That kills verification badges, blue checks, "Etsy Verified Human" — the first idea everyone in the room will have on Day 4. If we pitch platform-issued trust, we're pitching the thing the brief told us not to.

2. **The unfakeable list is the spec.** "Time, skill, care, presence." Anything we build should make at least one of those four *felt* by a buyer, not *claimed* by a seller.

3. **Loneliness is the hint.** They listed a loneliness report in a commerce challenge. They're pointing at connection-as-product, not authentication-as-product. Q3: *"buying becomes a connection between two people, rather than a transaction with a story attached."* That phrase — "a transaction with a story attached" — is them describing **Etsy today, critically.** That's a self-aware brief.

4. **The sponsor owns Buyer Experience.** Solve the buyer's felt experience of believing. Not seller tooling. Not fraud ops.

5. **They asked for a ritual or community practice, not just an app.** "An experience, a tool, a ritual, or a community practice." Three of four options are not software.

**Open, unanswered:** what does Etsy consider "a strong outcome"? They defined it on Day 1. → we need those notes.
