## The wound (why this matters)
Etsy is spending brand budget ("Celebrate Being Human", 2026) to claim *human* at the exact moment AI made *human* unprovable — while its real business bleed is **frequency**: the average buyer purchases ~3×/yr, ~50% are one-and-done, habitual buyers −11% YoY. New CEO Kruti Patel Goyal's Right-to-Win pillar is literally *"Keep Commerce Human."* The marketplace cannot currently enforce that promise.

## The reframe (our edge over every other team)
Every other team will pitch the romantic version — *help Gen Z connect with makers*. Our own discovery data kills that: connection features die on **seller apathy** ("almost no interaction at all with the customer"), buyers defect on **price**, and "authenticity" really means **"not a scam."** So the winning move is not a relationship product. It is an **un-fakeable, discovery-time, near-zero-seller-effort proof-of-human** that also gives a reason to return.

## The bet
**In the Making** — because handmade is made-to-order, we turn the *wait* into the product. When a Gen Z buyer orders, they receive 2–3 timestamped, camera-signed moments of the maker making **their specific piece** (started → in progress → done), rendered as an anticipation thread in-app.
- It is the **deepest possible proof-of-human** — you *watch it happen*, for you.
- It is a built-in **3× return loop** (reopen the app per ping) — proof and frequency from **one atom**.
- It demands **no DM / relationship burden** on the seller — capture during work they already do.

**Pitch spine:** *Etsy stops being a search box you visit on-need and becomes a set of real humans you follow, watch make your thing, and get introduced to by humans you already trust — proof and frequency from the same un-fakeable atom.*

## The system it grows into
The passive process clip is the **atom**. In the Making is the hero; the vision scales to:
- **Follow the Hands** — the clip becomes a *feed* (retention by subscription).
- **Trust Graph** — makers vouch for makers; realness propagates through humans, not a platform badge (brief Q4).

## Strategic fit
- Lands in **Dina Murphy's** exact domain (VP, Buyer Experience) — a buyer-side confidence layer that lifts frequency, not a seller-compliance burden.
- First **structural** answer to a decade of "fake handmade" press; operationalizes the "human connection" pillar; closes the Celebrate-Being-Human coherence gap.

## Success = (by Final Pitch, Fri Jul 24)
A working prototype of the **buyer-side "In the Making" thread** + a **seller-side one-tap capture** flow, tested with real Gen Z buyers and ≥1 real maker, with evidence on the crux question below.

## The 3 make-or-break unknowns (prototyping + testing, days 5–9)
1. **Seller effort at scale (THE crux).** Will real makers capture per-order moments without it becoming a chore they abandon? Test one-tap capture with a real seller at volume. Fallback if no: *first-purchase-only*, or *bank-once* reusable clips.
2. **Buyer pull.** Does the thread actually drive a return-open and a second purchase — or is it novelty they ignore? Test with real Gen Z.
3. **Proof legibility & fake-resistance.** Does a timestamped make-moment read as "real" in ~3 seconds, and does it survive improving AI video (12–18mo decay)? Is camera-signing (C2PA) needed, or is personalized raw process enough?

## Anti-goals (do NOT)
- Do **not** build a messaging/relationship product (dies on seller apathy).
- Do **not** propose blockchain/NFT provenance (dead category — a red flag in the room).
- Do **not** add mandatory seller chores; capture must be genuinely passive/optional.
- Do **not** sell "real" as a premium the buyer must reward at the comparison moment — design the moment so the comparison never happens.
